<p><p>Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) for electricity generation are categorized into two primary types: single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs) and multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWCNTs). SWCNTs consist of a single cylindrical layer of carbon atoms, offering superior electrical conductivity and flexibility, making them ideal for nanoelectronics and advanced sensors. In contrast, MWCNTs comprise multiple concentric layers, providing enhanced mechanical strength and stability. Both types have promising applications in energy storage, fuel cells, and supercapacitors, driving innovation in efficient electricity generation technologies.</p></p>
