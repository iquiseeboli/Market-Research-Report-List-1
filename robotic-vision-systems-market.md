<p><p>Robotic vision systems are categorized into 2D and 3D vision systems based on their imaging capabilities. 2D vision systems capture flat images, primarily used for object identification, alignment, and inspection in applications like quality control and assembly. In contrast, 3D vision systems provide depth perception, enabling tasks like spatial mapping and complex object manipulation. This technology is vital in automation, robotics, and industrial applications, enhancing accuracy and efficiency in manufacturing processes by allowing robots to "see" and interpret their environment.</p></p>
