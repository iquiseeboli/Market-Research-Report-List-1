<p><p>The geofencing market is primarily divided into two types: fixed geofencing and mobile geofencing. Fixed geofencing involves establishing virtual boundaries around a stationary location, allowing businesses to target users based on their proximity to specific physical sites, such as retail stores or events. In contrast, mobile geofencing focuses on tracking users' movements through their mobile devices, enabling real-time engagement and personalized marketing based on their location patterns. Both types enhance location-based services and improve customer interaction strategies.</p></p>
