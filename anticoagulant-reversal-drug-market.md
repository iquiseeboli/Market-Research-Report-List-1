<p><p>The anticoagulant reversal drug market features several key types, including prothrombin complex concentrates (PCCs), vitamin K, protamine, and tranexamic acid. PCCs are used to quickly reverse vitamin K antagonist effects, while vitamin K itself is essential for restoring normal coagulation. Protamine effectively neutralizes the anticoagulant effects of heparin. Additionally, tranexamic acid helps reduce bleeding by inhibiting fibrinolysis. Each type serves specific clinical scenarios, addressing the need for rapid reversal of anticoagulation in various medical situations.</p></p>
